<p><p>Ceramic nanocomposites refer to materials that consist of ceramic particles combined with another matrix material, such as metal or glass-ceramic. One type is the ceramic particles in metal matrix composites, where ceramic particles are added to a metal matrix, improving the mechanical properties of the material. Another type is carbon nanotube-reinforced glass-ceramic matrix composites, where carbon nanotubes are incorporated into a glass-ceramic matrix, enhancing its strength and electrical conductivity. High temperature superconducting ceramic nanocomposites possess enhanced superconducting properties at elevated temperatures. Lastly, the ceramic particle nanofluids market involves the utilization of ceramic particles dispersed in a liquid, which is used for heat transfer applications.</p></p>
