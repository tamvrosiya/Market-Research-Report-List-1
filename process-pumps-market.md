<p><p>Process pumps are used in various industries to transfer liquids or gases within a system. Axial flow pumps are designed to move fluids in a parallel flow, with the impeller pushing the fluid in a straight line. Centrifugal pumps use rotational energy to propel the fluid through the pump, creating centrifugal force. Multistage pumps have multiple impellers stacked together, providing increased pressure for pumping over long distances. Other pumps in the market may include diaphragm pumps, gear pumps, or peristaltic pumps, each designed for specific applications based on their unique pumping mechanisms.</p></p>
