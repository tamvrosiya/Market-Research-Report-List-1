<p><p>Short-pulse lasers refer to a type of lasers that emit ultra-short pulses of light, typically in the range of femtoseconds to picoseconds. These lasers are characterized by their extremely high peak power and short pulse duration, enabling them to perform precise material processing, spectroscopy, and medical imaging among other applications.</p><p>The short-pulse lasers market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for these lasers is driven by their wide range of applications in various industries, including healthcare, electronics, automotive, and aerospace. In the healthcare sector, short-pulse lasers are extensively used in ophthalmology, dermatology, and dentistry for surgeries and laser therapy. Moreover, the growing demand for high-precision material processing techniques in industries such as electronics and automotive is also fueling the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in laser technology, such as the development of diode-pumped solid-state (DPSS) lasers and fiber lasers, are driving the market expansion. These technological advancements have enhanced the reliability, efficiency, and versatility of short-pulse lasers, making them more suitable for diverse applications.</p><p>Additionally, the growing adoption of short-pulse lasers in scientific research and academic institutions is contributing to market growth.
